Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Sprint4 Cultural Blog

Research Neuroplasticity I value the understanding of Neuroplasticity as a physical representation of the mind responding to a flexible mindset. This understanding I believe is crucial in when we think of the many negative effects one might face either within a stagnent headspace or being observed by one with this particular headspace. In a world of growing acceptance for difference not just in a persons make up from birth but the ever evolving transitions of life this will have to be a widely accepted scientifical understanding. I currently relate to this idea with my predicament as I learn to shift my thought processes to accommodate for this very unique way of thinking in tech. Visualising the neurons making connections with each new lesson is helping to build a physical connection with my learning. Growth mindset is one that I gravitate towards as a new parent. The idea that a simple change in approach, in positive engagement and building a healthy relationship with failure is hugely encouraging to learn of the huge successes associated with this. I like to think about the kindness we have to apply to our minds, the gentle approach that fosters strength rather then fear and fustration. In my learning I hope to remind myself of the changing nature of learning and the positive complications that come with that.
